Multimodal Model Evaluation and Interpretability

Evaluating how audio and vision foundation models represent semantic information across modalities.

Description

This project investigates whether modern vision and audio foundation models develop similar internal representations of semantic concepts. While most multimodal research focuses on language–vision alignment, direct comparisons between auditory and visual models remain limited. This work analyzes whether shared semantic structure emerges across modalities even when models are trained independently.

Data

A controlled multimodal dataset was constructed using audio clips and captions from the AudioCaps dataset. Corresponding images were generated using Stable Diffusion to ensure consistent semantic content across both audio and visual inputs.

Method

Embeddings are extracted from pretrained foundation models (BEATs for audio and DINOv2 for vision). Representation similarity metrics such as Centered Kernel Alignment (CKA) and Representational Similarity Analysis (RSA) are used to measure alignment across layers and model architectures.
